## New findings
The durable finding was not a new abstract taxonomy claim.
It was a practical stop-rule refinement for USB cases:

```text
transfer exists
  != relevant completion truth
  != first completion consumer proved
  != later visible consequence truth
```

What this clarified for the protocol/firmware branch:
- `usb_submit_urb(...)` or `libusb_submit_transfer(...)` visibility is weaker than proving the relevant completion
- usbmon completion visibility is weaker than proving the first callback/parser/router that gives the completed transfer meaning
- callback existence is weaker than proving callback-side consumer ownership
- endpoint/direction/status/`actual_length` are useful truth objects, but still weaker than a first trustworthy completion-owned consumer plus one later visible consequence
